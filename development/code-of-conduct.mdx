The Zulip team and community is made up of professionals and volunteers from all over the world, working on every aspect of the mission, including mentorship, teaching, and connecting people.

Diversity is one of our huge strengths, but it can also lead to communication issues and unhappiness. To that end, we have ground rules that we ask people to adhere to.

## Expected Behavior

The following behaviors are expected and requested of all community members:

- **Participate** - In doing so, you contribute to the health and longevity of the community
- **Exercise consideration and respect** in your speech and actions
- **Attempt collaboration before conflict** - Assume good faith
- **Refrain from demeaning, discriminatory, or harassing behavior and speech**
- **Take action** or alert community leaders if you notice violations of this code
- **Be respectful** to all patrons when community events are at shared venues

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable:

### Discriminatory Language

Jokes or derogatory language that singles out members of any:
- Race, ethnicity, culture, or national origin
- Immigration status
- Social and economic class
- Educational level or language proficiency
- Sex, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression
- Age, size, or family status
- Political belief or religion
- Mental and physical ability

### Threatening Behavior

- Violence, threats of violence, or violent language directed against another person
- Disseminating or threatening to disseminate another person's personal information
- Personal insults of any sort

### Inappropriate Content

- Posting or displaying sexually explicit or violent material
- Inappropriate photography or recording
- Unwelcome sexual attention, including sexualized comments or jokes
- Inappropriate touching or groping
- Unwelcomed sexual advances

### Disruptive Behavior

- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following (online or in person)
- Sustained disruption of community events, including talks and presentations
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors

## AI Policy

**Do not post AI-generated messages** - we want to read your own genuine expression of your thoughts. It's fine to use whatever tools you like for help with spelling, grammar, or translation.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Harassment and other code of conduct violations reduce the value of the community for everyone. If someone makes you or anyone else feel unsafe or unwelcome, please report it to:

**zulip-code-of-conduct@googlegroups.com**

You can make a report either personally or anonymously.

### Consequences

If a community member engages in unacceptable behavior, the community organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, up to and including:

- Temporary ban from the community
- Permanent expulsion from the community without warning
- Refund denial in the case of paid events

### Support for Affected Members

If someone outside the development community engages in unacceptable behavior that affects someone in the community, we still want to know. Community organizers can:

- Support the people affected
- Reduce the chance of similar violations in the future
- Take any direct action within their control

### Confidentiality

All reports will be kept confidential. In some cases, we may determine that a public statement is needed, but the identities of all victims and reporters will remain confidential unless those individuals instruct us otherwise.

## Scope

We expect all community participants to abide by this Code of Conduct in:

- All community venues, online and in-person
- All private communications pertaining to community business
- Situations outside community activities when such behavior has the potential to adversely affect the safety and well-being of community members

This applies to:

- Contributors (paid or otherwise)
- Sponsors
- Other guests

## Moderating the Zulip Community

Anyone can help moderate the Zulip community by ensuring folks are aware of the community guidelines and Code of Conduct.

### How You Can Help

**Be Friendly**

Welcome folks, thank them for their feedback, ideas and effort, and try to keep the atmosphere warm. New participants who feel accepted are likely to treat others the same way.

**Cite Guidelines**

Be familiar with the [community guidelines](https://zulip.com/development-community/), and cite them liberally when a user violates them. Be polite but firm.

Example:

<Note>
@user please note that there is no need to @-mention core contributors when you ask a question. As noted in the [guidelines](https://zulip.com/development-community/):

> Use @-mentions sparingly… there is generally no need to @-mention a core contributor unless you need their timely attention.
</Note>

**Move Misplaced Messages**

If you see a message posted in the wrong place, go ahead and move it if you have permissions to do so. Leave the "Send automated notice" option enabled to make it clear what happened.

**Report Spam**

If a user is posting spam, [report it](https://zulip.com/help/report-a-message) to community moderators. They will change the user's name to `<name> (spammer)`, deactivate them, and delete any spam messages.

**Address Inappropriate Tone**

If you see someone being mean or rude, point out that their tone is inappropriate and ask them to communicate respectfully in the future. If you don't feel comfortable doing so, ask a member of Zulip's core team.

**Assume the Best**

Try to assume the best intentions from others and stick with a friendly and positive tone even when someone's behavior is poor or disrespectful. Everyone has bad days.
