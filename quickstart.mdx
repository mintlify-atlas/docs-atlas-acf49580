## Step 1: Set Up Your Account

### Joining an Existing Organization

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check your email">
    Look for an invitation email from Zulip. It will contain a link to join your organization.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click the invitation link">
    This will take you to the registration page for your organization.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Enter your name and create a password. You can also sign in with Google, GitHub, or other configured authentication methods.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Creating a New Organization

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit Zulip Cloud">
    Go to [zulip.com/new](https://zulip.com/new) to create a new organization.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    Provide your work email address to get started.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm your email">
    Check your inbox for a confirmation email and click the link.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set up your organization">
    Choose your organization name, URL, and complete the setup wizard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Prefer self-hosting?** Check out our [installation guide](/self-hosting/installation) to deploy Zulip on your own infrastructure.
</Tip>

## Step 2: Complete Your Profile

Once you're logged in, personalize your account:

<Steps>

  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in your profile details">
    Add information about yourself:
    
    - Your full name
    - Your role or title
    - Timezone (important for async collaboration)
    - Custom profile fields (if configured by your org)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set up your preferences">
    Navigate to **Personal settings** → **Preferences** to customize:
    
    - Theme (light/dark)
    - Language
    - Emoji style
    - Font size
    - Keyboard shortcuts
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **First-time tip**: We recommend starting with the web or desktop app to get familiar with Zulip before using mobile.
</Info>

## Step 4: Understand Topics

**Topics** are what make Zulip special. Every message in Zulip belongs to a channel and has a topic.

<div className="bg-gradient-to-r from-[#5c9cfc]/10 to-[#747bfc]/10 rounded-lg p-6 my-4 border-l-4 border-[#5c9cfc]">
  <h3 className="text-lg font-semibold mb-2">What's a topic?</h3>
  <p>
    A topic is like the subject line of an email. It describes what a conversation is about. This lets multiple conversations happen in the same channel without getting mixed up.
  </p>
</div>

### Example: The #general channel

Instead of all messages in #general being one big stream, you might have:

- **Topic: "Welcome"** - Introductions from new team members
- **Topic: "Lunch plans"** - Coordinating today's lunch
- **Topic: "Bug #1234"** - Discussing a specific issue
- **Topic: "Q1 Planning"** - Planning discussion that spans several days

Each conversation stays organized and easy to follow!

<Tip>
  **Coming from Slack or Teams?** Topics in Zulip replace the "threads" feature, but they work much better because they're front and center, not hidden in a sidebar.
</Tip>

## Step 5: Subscribe to Channels

Channels (also called streams) organize your organization's conversations by team, project, or topic area.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the channel browser">
    Click the **#** icon in the left sidebar or press `Q` to open the channel list.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Browse available channels">
    You'll see:
    - **Subscribed**: Channels you're already subscribed to
    - **All channels**: Public channels you can join
    
    Each channel shows its description and number of subscribers.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Subscribe to channels">
    Click **Subscribe** next to any channel that interests you.
    
    Recommended channels to start:
    - **#general** - Organization-wide discussions
    - **#new members** - Questions and help for new users
    - Team or project channels relevant to your work
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Customize notifications">
    For each channel, you can:
    - Mute it (stay subscribed but don't get notifications)
    - Set notification preferences
    - Pin it to the top of your sidebar
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Don't worry about subscribing to too many channels. Zulip's topic-based organization makes it easy to scan and catch up on what matters.
</Note>

## Step 6: Send Your First Message

Let's send your first message! We'll start with a channel message.

### Compose a Channel Message

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start composing">
    Click **New topic** in the compose box at the bottom of the screen, or press `C`.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose your channel">
    Type the channel name (e.g., "general") or select from the dropdown.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter a topic">
    Give your message a descriptive topic. For your first message, try "Introduction" or "Hello from [Your Name]".
    
    Good topic names are specific:
    - ✅ "Bug in login page"
    - ✅ "Q1 budget review"
    - ✅ "Redesign feedback"
    - ❌ "Question" (too vague)
    - ❌ "Hi" (not descriptive)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Write your message">
    Type your message in the compose box. You can use:
    
    - **Bold** with `**bold**`
    - *Italic* with `*italic*`
    - `Code` with `` `code` ``
    - Emoji with `:smile:` :smile:
    - And [much more](/guides/message-formatting)!
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Send">
    Press **Enter** (or **Ctrl+Enter** depending on your settings) to send!
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Try a Direct Message

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a DM">
    Click **New direct message** or press `X`.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose recipients">
    Type the name of one or more people to message. You can send DMs to individuals or create group DMs with up to 8 people.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Write and send">
    Type your message and press **Enter** to send.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **Keyboard shortcuts are your friend!** Press `?` to see all available shortcuts. Here are a few essential ones:
  - `C` - New channel message
  - `X` - New direct message  
  - `R` - Reply to selected message
  - `S` - Narrow to topic
</Info>

## Step 7: Read and Reply to Messages

Now let's learn how to navigate conversations:

### Finding Conversations

<Accordion title="Inbox View (Recommended for new users)">
  The **Inbox** shows all your unread messages organized by conversation:
  
  1. Click **Inbox** in the left sidebar
  2. See your unread messages grouped by channel and topic
  3. Click any conversation to read it
  4. Mark conversations as read individually or all at once
  
  This is the best way to catch up on what you've missed!
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Recent Conversations">
  See all recent activity across your organization:
  
  1. Click **Recent conversations** in the left sidebar
  2. Browse topics with recent messages
  3. Click any topic to jump into that conversation
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Channel Sidebar">
  The left sidebar shows your subscribed channels:
  
  1. Click a channel name to see all messages in that channel
  2. Click a topic name to narrow to just that conversation
  3. See unread counts next to each channel and topic
</Accordion>

### Replying to Messages

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a message">
    Click on the message you want to reply to, or use arrow keys to navigate.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Press R to reply">
    The compose box will automatically fill in the channel and topic from the selected message.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Write your reply">
    Type your response. Your message will appear in the same topic, keeping the conversation organized.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Mention someone (optional)">
    Type `@` followed by their name to notify them:
    - `@Alice` notifies Alice
    - `@**all**` notifies everyone in the channel (use sparingly!)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  **Important**: Replying keeps the conversation in the same topic. If you want to start a new conversation, use `C` for a new topic instead of `R` to reply.
</Warning>

Now that you've sent your first messages, explore these powerful features:

  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Emoji Reactions" icon="face-smile">
    Hover over a message and click the emoji icon to add a reaction. Quick way to acknowledge without a full reply!

  <Card title="Quote Messages" icon="quote-left">
    Click the three-dot menu on any message and select "Quote and reply" to include context in your response.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Step 9: Configure Notifications

Get notified the way you work:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open notification settings">
    Go to **Personal settings** → **Notifications**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose your notification style">
    - **Desktop notifications**: Pop-ups on your computer
    - **Mobile push notifications**: Alerts on your phone
    - **Email notifications**: Messages sent to email
    - **Audible notifications**: Sound alerts
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Customize by channel">
    Set different notification preferences for each channel:
    - **All messages**: Get notified for every message
    - **@-mentions only**: Only when someone mentions you
    - **Muted**: Stay subscribed but don't get notifications
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set up follows">
    Follow specific topics to get notified for all messages in that conversation, even without being mentioned.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Recommendation**: Start with @-mentions only for most channels. Follow specific topics you want to track closely. You can always adjust as you learn your preferences!
</Tip>

## Common Tasks

### Edit a Message

1. Hover over your message
2. Click the pencil icon (or press `E` when the message is selected)
3. Make your changes
4. Press **Enter** to save

<Info>
  Edited messages show an "EDITED" indicator with edit history. Administrators can configure how long users can edit messages.
</Info>

### Move a Message to a Different Topic

1. Click the three-dot menu on the message
2. Select **Move messages**
3. Choose to move just this message or all messages in the topic
4. Enter the new channel and/or topic name

### Mute a Topic

If a conversation isn't relevant to you:

1. Hover over the topic in the left sidebar
2. Click the three-dot menu
3. Select **Mute topic**

Muted topics won't appear in your inbox or unread counts.

### Format Your Messages

Click the **A** icon in the compose box to see formatting options:

- Bold, italic, strikethrough
- Links
- Bulleted and numbered lists
- Code blocks
- Emoji picker
- File uploads

Or type Markdown directly:

```markdown
**Bold text**
*Italic text*
`Code`
[Link](https://example.com)

```python
def hello():
    print("Code block")
```

- Bulleted list

```

## Tips for Success

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use descriptive topic names">
    Good topics make conversations easy to find later:
    - ✅ "Budget Q1 2024"
    - ✅ "Bug: Login fails on Firefox"
    - ✅ "Hiring: Backend engineer role"
    - ❌ "Question"
    - ❌ "FYI"
    - ❌ "Meeting"
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Don't worry about interrupting">
    Each topic is its own conversation. You're not interrupting by starting a new topic in the same channel!
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use threads (topics) instead of DMs">
    Channel discussions are searchable and help others learn. Use DMs for truly private conversations.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Check Recent Conversations">
    The Recent Conversations view is great for seeing what's happening across your organization.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Master keyboard shortcuts">
    Keyboard shortcuts make Zulip incredibly fast. Press `?` to see them all, and learn a few at a time.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
